limoncello

Ah, limoncello, sweet nectar of the Gods. I remember first tasting limoncello as a young girl at family dinners. And of course, when you're young and your Italian uncles give you a taste of wine or liqueur, it's usually a healthy sip given under the watchful eye of your Mom.Sipping wine, limoncello, or any other 'cello is as much a cultural thing in an Italian household (or in my case Italian-American household) as eating Italian food, or sharing the same kitchen towel around the dinner table to wipe your mouth (sorry, I know how this sounds), and lingering after dinner over desserts, fruits, nuts, and lively conversation.Drinking limoncello in Italy is a bucket list item for sure. When I first went looking for an Italian limoncello recipe, I found dozens, some with added cream or sugar. But eventually I worked out my own tried and true recipe that I'm sharing with you!
